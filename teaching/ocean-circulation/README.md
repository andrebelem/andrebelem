# Ocean Circulation: Surface and Deep Ocean Dynamics

**Last updated:** 2026-06-14

This page contains teaching materials and interactive resources used in the undergraduate lecture **"Introduction to Oceanography"** for first-year Geophysics students at the **Fluminense Federal University (UFF), Brazil**.

The lecture introduces the fundamental mechanisms that drive ocean circulation, from wind-driven surface currents to the global thermohaline circulation, emphasizing the role of the ocean in Earth's climate system and the use of real-world oceanographic datasets.

---

## Topics Covered

### Surface Circulation

- Wind-driven circulation
- Coriolis effect
- Ocean gyres
- Major ocean currents
- Ekman transport
- Coastal upwelling

### Deep Circulation

- Ocean stratification
- Temperature and salinity controls on density
- Water masses
- North Atlantic Deep Water (NADW)
- Antarctic Bottom Water (AABW)
- Thermohaline circulation
- Atlantic Meridional Overturning Circulation (AMOC)

### Ocean and Climate

- Meridional heat transport
- Ocean heat storage
- Climate regulation
- Ocean-climate interactions
- Potential impacts of climate change on circulation patterns

---

## Learning Objectives

After completing this lecture, students should be able to:

- Explain why ocean circulation exists.
- Describe the role of ocean circulation in global climate regulation.
- Distinguish between surface and deep circulation mechanisms.
- Explain the Coriolis effect and Ekman transport.
- Describe the formation of major water masses.
- Interpret basic temperature and salinity profiles.
- Explore and visualize oceanographic datasets using online tools.

## Interactive Resources

### Earth Nullschool

Real-time visualization of atmospheric and oceanic fields.

Useful for:

- Surface currents
- Wind patterns
- Waves
- Sea surface temperature

https://earth.nullschool.net

---

### OSCAR (Ocean Surface Current Analysis Real-Time)

Global surface current estimates derived from satellite observations.

Useful for:

- Surface circulation
- Major ocean currents
- Current velocity comparisons

https://www.earthdata.nasa.gov/data/projects/oscar

---

### NASA Worldview

Interactive satellite imagery portal.

Useful for:

- Sea Surface Temperature (SST)
- Ocean color
- Chlorophyll-a
- Sea ice

https://worldview.earthdata.nasa.gov

---

### ODV Web (Ocean Data View)

Interactive exploration of oceanographic observations.

Useful for:

- Vertical profiles
- Temperature sections
- Salinity sections
- Water mass identification

https://odv.awi.de

---

### Copernicus Marine Viewer

Interactive visualization of three-dimensional ocean fields.

Useful for:

- Surface currents
- Deep currents
- Temperature
- Salinity
- Ocean forecasts

https://marine.copernicus.eu/access-data/myocean-viewer

---

### Global Drifter Program

Real-time drifting buoy observations maintained by NOAA.

Useful for:

- Lagrangian oceanography
- Surface circulation
- Ocean current tracking

https://www.aoml.noaa.gov/global-drifter-program

---

### Argo Program

Global network of autonomous profiling floats.

Useful for:

- Temperature profiles
- Salinity profiles
- Ocean stratification
- Water mass studies

https://fleetmonitoring.euro-argo.eu
